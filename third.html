Third Trimester

  • Protein: Important for baby's growth and development.
    • Foods:
      • Lean meats (like poultry and fish)
      • Legumes (beans and lentils)
      • Tofu
      • Nuts and seeds
  • Calcium: Essential for bone development of the baby.
    • Foods:
      • Dairy products (milk, yogurt, cheese)
      • Fortified plant-based milks (like almond or soy milk)
      • Leafy green vegetables (like spinach and kale)
      • Calcium-fortified foods (like tofu)
  • Iron: Helps prevent anemia and supports oxygen transport in the blood.
    • Foods:
      • Lean red meat
      • Poultry
      • Fish
      • Beans
      • Lentils
      • Iron-fortified cereals
      • Leafy green vegetables
  • Omega-3 fatty acids: Important for brain and eye development of the baby.
    • Foods:
      • Fatty fish (like salmon and sardines)
      • Flaxseeds
      • Chia seeds
      • Walnuts
      • Omega-3 fortified eggs
  • Folate (Folic Acid): Essential for preventing neural tube defects.
    • Foods:
      • Leafy green vegetables (like spinach and broccoli)
      • Citrus fruits (like oranges and grapefruits)
      • Beans
      • Fortified cereals
  • Fiber: Helps prevent constipation, which can be common during pregnancy.
    • Foods:
      • Whole grains (like oats, brown rice, and whole wheat bread)
      • Fruits (like apples and berries)
      • Vegetables (like carrots and peas)
      • Legumes
  • Fluids: Important for maintaining hydration and supporting amniotic fluid levels.
    • Drink plenty of water throughout the day, and consider adding herbal teas or fresh fruit juices (in moderation).
  • Snacks: Healthy snacks can help maintain energy levels and satisfy cravings.
    • Foods:
      • Fruits
      • Yogurt
      • Nuts
      • Whole grain crackers with cheese
      • Vegetable sticks with hummus
