First Trimester

    • Folate (Folic Acid): Crucial for preventing neural tube defects in the baby. Sources include:
      • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
      • Citrus fruits
      • Beans
      • Fortified cereals
    • Iron: Needed for the increased blood volume and to prevent anemia. Good sources include:
      • Lean red meat
      • Poultry
      • Fish
      • Beans
      • Fortified cereals
    • Calcium: Important for the baby's bone development. Sources include:
      • Dairy products (milk, cheese, yogurt)
      • Fortified plant-based milks
      • Leafy greens
    • Protein: Essential for the baby's growth. Include:
      • Lean meats (chicken, turkey)
      • Fish
      • Eggs
      • Beans
      • Nuts and seeds
    • Healthy Fats: Omega-3 fatty acids are important for the baby's brain and eye development. Sources include:
      • Fatty fish (salmon, trout)
      • Flaxseeds
      • Chia seeds
      • Walnuts
    • Whole Grains: Provide fiber and energy. Choose:
      • Whole wheat bread
      • Brown rice
      • Quinoa
      • Oats
      • Whole grain pasta
    • Fruits and Vegetables: Rich in vitamins, minerals, and fiber. Aim for a variety of:
      • Fruits (e.g., berries, apples)
      • Vegetables (e.g., carrots, broccoli)
    • Fluids: Stay well-hydrated with:
      • Water
      • Herbal teas
      • Fresh fruit juices (in moderation)
    • Small, Frequent Meals: Help manage nausea and maintain steady blood sugar levels.
    • Supplements: Depending on individual needs and as recommended by healthcare providers, prenatal vitamins containing folic acid, iron, calcium, and other essential nutrients may be recommended.
